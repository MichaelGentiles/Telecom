Communication Systems Summary

System Description Strengths Limitations
Copper Systems Transmits data as electrical signals through metal wires like twisted pair and coaxial cables. Common in legacy telephone and DSL networks. Cost-effective for short distances, widely deployed, supports voice and basic data. Slower speeds, limited bandwidth, signal degradation over long distances.
Wireless Systems Uses electromagnetic waves (radio, microwave) to transmit data through the air. Enables mobile and remote connectivity. Flexible, no physical cables needed, supports mobility, easy to expand coverage. Prone to interference, security concerns, generally lower speed than wired systems.
Fiber Optics Uses pulses of light through glass or plastic strands to transmit data. Ideal for high-speed, long-distance communication. Extremely fast, high bandwidth, low signal loss, immune to electromagnetic interference. Higher installation costs, more fragile than copper, requires specialized equipment.
