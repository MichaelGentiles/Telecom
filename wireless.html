Wireless Systems

Modern telecommunications systems transmit data through radio waves instead of physical wiring.

Description

Wireless systems use electromagnetic waves—such as radio, microwave, or infrared—to transmit data through the air. This eliminates the need for physical cables and enables mobility and flexible connectivity.

Function

These systems are commonly used for mobile networks, Wi-Fi, satellite communication, and Bluetooth. They support data and voice transmission over short to long distances, depending on the technology used.
